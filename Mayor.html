Mayor Volunteering

After hearing from my friend that her dad was running for mayor, I went campaigning with them to see what it was all about. For 2 hours, we went around the neighborhood explaining what Mr. Goel was campaigning for. When I first read, why he was campaigning, I one hundred percent agreed and really felt that he could improve Dublin. Some of Mr. Goel's ideas were to pause housing, solve the school crisis, and bring back business. While I was campaigning, I was able to make connections with the people I was talking to. For example, when I saw parents, I was able to communicate what it is like having to get up early to take the bus and get stuck in traffic. Trust me, sitting in a bus for an hour is not fun. When it comes to business, I saw my dad drive through commute every day for me and my brother. The only question I had for him was, don't you ever get bored of all this? His response, I do, but when I know I have to do it, what is the point in complaining. Something that anyone who travels on I580 and passes Dublin can relate to is the crazy housing! Whenever I see the hills, or should I say what used to be hills, I can only think that we need to stop! Everywhere I turn, there are houses, it is like some disease spread. Communicating all of these points to people who would understand me made the job a lot easier and smoother. This overall experience made me more confident and knowledgable about what is happening around me.
