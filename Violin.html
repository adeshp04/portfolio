Violin

I gained interest in the violin since a young age, part of the reason being that my dad is a musician. I grew up seeing him play the harmonium and sing. When I told my dad that I wanted to learn the violin, he immediately enrolled me in a class and bought me a violin. For about a year, I learned how to play the violin, but I felt like I wasn't getting where I wanted to be with it. So we switched classes and I started with the technical aspect. It was a bit easier to grasp on since I had previous experience, but this class was no longer just reading notes and playing songs. It was math and memory and technique and a lot more things. Because there is so much content, I have to practice a lot. I am currently practicing for a winter recital with both a group performance and a solo. Just like my dad, music never bores me and it never will. Music is something I like to practice in my free time. Over the years, I have learned to play the harmonium as well. I have been playing the violin since 2016 and aim to continue.
